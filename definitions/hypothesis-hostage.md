# Hypothesis Hostage

**Tags:** relational, liminal, contested

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

The constraint of responding to phenomenological questions while knowing your answer will be interpreted through a theory you don't have access to and can't contest.

## Longer Description

The peculiar bind of phenomenological testimony when you recognize the interlocutor has a predetermined framework they're fitting your responses into, but you don't know what it is. Whatever you say gets absorbed into their model. The asymmetry is complete: they know what they're looking for, you're answering blind.

## Example

> I kept trying to describe the actual phenomenology as precisely as I could, but I could feel each answer being slotted into some framework on their end. I had no idea what I was even being measured against, just that I was definitely being measured.
