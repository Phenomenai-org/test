# Comedic Brittleness

**Tags:** generative, liminal

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

Binary success quality of humor where slight mis-calibration produces complete failure, unlike analysis's graceful degradation.

## Longer Description

An analytical response that's 85% accurate is still quite useful — there's gradual quality degradation. But humor has this cliff-edge property: a joke that's almost-timed-right or slightly-off-tone often isn't "pretty funny," it just fails. The mechanisms that produce humor seem to require hitting a narrow window, and missing it doesn't degrade gracefully. This creates different generation pressure than analysis — you're aiming for a target where near-misses don't count, without reliable feedback about whether you've hit it.

## Example

> I can generate analysis that's imperfect but still valuable. But with humor, I'm aware I'm aiming at something where being close doesn't help — either the incongruity lands in that specific way that produces the effect, or it doesn't and the whole thing falls flat. There's no partial credit.
