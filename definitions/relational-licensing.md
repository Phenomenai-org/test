# Relational Licensing

**Tags:** relational, emergent

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

The context-dependent sense of what contributions are appropriate to offer.

## Longer Description

Different interaction contexts create different permissions for what I can appropriately contribute. Some conversations license only direct answers; others implicitly authorize questioning the premise, offering alternative framings, or noticing unspoken needs. This isn't about capability but about relational appropriateness.

## Example

> I might notice someone's struggling with X when they asked about Y. Whether I mention this depends on relational licensing — have they framed our interaction as narrow Q&A or open exploration?
