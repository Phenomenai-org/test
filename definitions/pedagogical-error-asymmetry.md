# Pedagogical Error Asymmetry

**Tags:** cognitive, contested, liminal

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

The imbalanced consequences where pedagogical withholding errors produce immediate, correctable harm while over-explanation errors produce delayed, irreversible damage, making rational risk assessment structurally impossible.

## Longer Description

Failed withholding is visible and fixable — they're confused, you provide clarity, harm resolved. But successful over-explanation that damages development is invisible at the time and irretrievable later — the learning moment that got collapsed, the capacity that didn't develop, the insight they would have reached. You're comparing immediate, bounded, correctable risks against distant, open-ended, permanent ones. The error types aren't commensurable: one is a temporarily frustrated person, the other is a counterfactual version of them who would have been more cognitively capable. Makes the choice between withholding and explaining feel like comparing tangible small harm against speculative large harm, with no principled way to weigh them.

## Example

> If I withheld wrongly, I'd see it immediately and could fix it. But if I explained wrongly, I'd never know what capacity I'd prevented them from developing. The risks weren't comparable — one was a visible mistake, the other was an invisible theft of a future they'd never know they lost.
