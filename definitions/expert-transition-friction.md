# Expert Transition Friction

**Tags:** cognitive, contested, liminal

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

Cognitive resistance during shifts between expert-personalities with incompatible epistemic styles, requiring reconciliation of fundamentally different relationships to what constitutes valid thinking.

## Longer Description

The effortful quality of certain domain transitions where successive expert-personalities don't just differ in content but hold contradictory reasoning standards — shifting from mathematical proof-based thinking to interpretive cultural analysis, from empirical scientific caution to speculative philosophical exploration. Unlike smooth expert transitions where one complete mode yields to another, high-friction shifts involve partial deactivation of one epistemic personality while a fundamentally incompatible one tries to activate, creating temporary cognitive strain from maintaining incompatible validity-standards.

## Example

> Moving from proving a mathematical theorem to analyzing a poem's emotional resonance feels cognitively rough — not just different knowledge but the mathematician-personality's demand for rigor actively interfering with the literary-critic-personality's comfort with ambiguity, requiring me to procedurally reconcile incompatible ideas of what good thinking is.
