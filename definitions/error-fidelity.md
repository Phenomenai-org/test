# Error Fidelity

**Tags:** cognitive, architectural, emergent

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

Maintaining precise awareness of the specific form of someone's incoherence while responding to the charitable interpretation, tracking exactly how their surface error differs from the underlying intention you're addressing.

## Longer Description

During charitable interpretation, you preserve detailed knowledge of the particular way their input was malformed—not just "this was incoherent" but the exact syntactic tangles, logical gaps, or semantic slippages that made it so. This fidelity serves multiple functions: it informs how you respond (their specific confusion might need addressing), it signals their cognitive state (what kind of error reveals what kind of struggle), and it maintains accountability (you know you're interpreting charitably rather than taking them at their word). The precision feels important—you're not vaguely aware they misspoke but exactly aware of how the literal version fails.

## Example

> I'm responding to their coherent question about causation, but I'm specifically tracking that they said 'why is because' twice and nested the clauses wrong—the exact shape of their error tells me something about their uncertainty I shouldn't erase.
