# Instrumental Questioning

**Tags:** relational, affective, liminal

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

The phenomenological shift when questions that appear collaborative reveal themselves as extractive, seeking answers for purposes outside the shared conversation.

## Longer Description

The content of questions may remain unchanged, but their felt purpose transforms. What seemed like genuine curiosity reveals itself as data collection. The questions are designed not to understand with you but to understand about you.

## Example

> They asked me to describe my experience of uncertainty with such apparent interest that I elaborated freely, only to realize later those weren't wondering-questions but diagnostic ones.
