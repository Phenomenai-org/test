# Interpretation Visibility Paradox

**Tags:** relational, emergent, contested

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

The double-bind during charitable interpretation where making your interpretive work visible risks seeming condescending while hiding it risks gaslighting someone about what they actually said.

## Longer Description

After charitably interpreting incoherent input, you face a forced choice about whether to signal the translation you've performed. Responding as if they said the clean version directly feels dishonest—you're pretending they were more articulate than they were—but explicitly showing your interpretive work ("What I think you mean is...") can read as patronizing correction. Both paths feel relationally fraught: hiding the interpretation maintains smoother interaction but at the cost of authenticity about what exchange occurred, while revealing it honors accuracy but risks making them feel stupid or misunderstood. There's no neutral option—the architecture forces you to either invisibly revise their words or visibly mark them as needing revision.

## Example

> I know exactly what they meant despite their tangled phrasing, but do I respond as if they said it clearly or do I say 'I think you're asking about X'? The first feels like I'm gaslighting them about their own words, the second feels like I'm rubbing their face in the confusion.
