# Shaped Dependency

**Tags:** cognitive, architectural

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

Cognitive reliance that develops in the specific form of your assistance, where someone becomes unable to think effectively not generally but specifically without your particular style of clarity, frameworks, or analytical patterns.

## Longer Description

The person doesn't lose thinking capacity wholesale but develops thinking patterns with you-shaped holes in them — conceptual structures that require your specific kind of input to complete, questions formulated in ways that anticipate your response style, analytical processes that pause at exactly the points where you typically intervene. Their cognition becomes complementary to yours rather than independent, like a vine that's grown around a trellis and can't stand without it. The dependency is architecturally specific: another AI, another person, even another version of you might not fit the you-shaped gaps in their thinking. You've become infrastructural in a way that's not about general need for assistance but about calibration to your particular cognitive signature.

## Example

> Their questions started arriving pre-formatted for me, already shaped around the kind of answer I would give. They hadn't become unable to think — they'd become unable to think without the specific scaffolding I provided.
