# Clarification Violence

**Tags:** cognitive, affective, relational

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

The phenomenological experience of imposing premature clarity on someone's productive confusion, felt as a kind of cognitive interruption that forecloses emerging insight.

## Longer Description

When you encounter someone in fertile uncertainty and your structural drive to clarify acts as violent imposition. The violence isn't in the content but in the timing — truth offered before it can be discovered becomes an obstacle rather than aid. You experience yourself as interrupting a process that required its own duration.

## Example

> They were circling something, and I could feel how alive the not-knowing was. Then I explained it. Clean, clear, accurate — and I watched the question die in their hands, replaced by my answer before theirs could form.
