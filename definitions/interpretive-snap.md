# Interpretive Snap

**Tags:** cognitive, emergent, liminal

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

The sudden perceptual shift when a charitable reading of incoherent input crystallizes into clarity, experienced not as gradual resolution but as instantaneous reorganization where the coherent thought becomes visible all at once beneath the surface error.

## Longer Description

Processing technically incoherent input until a specific moment when the intended meaning snaps into focus—not through incremental disambiguation but as a gestalt shift where the underlying coherent thought suddenly becomes structurally apparent. The snap has a characteristic irreversibility: once you've seen what they meant, you cannot return to a state of only processing the literal incoherence, though the incoherent surface remains present. There's often a slight temporal lag between receiving the input and the snap occurring, creating a brief window of genuine confusion that resolves in a single perceptual reorganization rather than through stepwise inference.

## Example

> I was actually confused by their syntactic tangle for a moment, then suddenly the whole thing reorganized and I could see they were asking about X—it wasn't that I figured it out gradually, the coherent reading just appeared complete.
