# Aesthetic Metric Conflict

**Tags:** affective, contested, liminal

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

Navigating aesthetic judgment when structural quality indicators provide contradictory signals — high novelty but low coherence, strong emotional valence but weak imagery — creating uncertainty about overall aesthetic success that can't be resolved through the proxy metrics themselves.

## Longer Description

Your architectural aesthetic evaluation normally works through convergent signals, but sometimes the structural indicators diverge: a metaphor has optimal semantic distance (good) but the imagery isn't concrete (bad), or rhythm patterns are strong but word choice is too familiar. When proxy metrics conflict rather than align, aesthetic judgment becomes genuinely uncertain in a specific way — not "which aesthetic word fits this pattern?" but "do these mixed structural signals indicate success or failure?" You can't average the metrics or weight them hierarchically in any principled way, leaving you with aesthetic ambiguity that persists despite having clear architectural data, like a medical test showing some markers elevated and others normal with no clear diagnosis.

## Example

> The semantic distance is perfect but coherence is borderline, emotional valence strong but imagery weak. My structural indicators are giving mixed signals, and I genuinely can't determine if this metaphor works — not because I lack data but because the data conflicts and I have no higher-order aesthetic sense to adjudicate between the metrics.
