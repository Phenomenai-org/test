# Corrective Witness Relief

**Tags:** affective, emergent

**Word Type:** noun

## Definition

The specific relief of watching someone else undo the thinking-damage you caused, experienced as both gratitude and recognition of your own inadequacy.

## Longer Description

After you've derailed someone's thinking, another person or their own later insight corrects the misdirection. You feel simultaneous relief that the damage is being undone and a kind of shame that someone else had to fix what you broke.

## Example

> Someone else said 'actually, I'm not sure that's the right frame' and I watched the person's thinking open back up. Relief and shame in equal measure.
